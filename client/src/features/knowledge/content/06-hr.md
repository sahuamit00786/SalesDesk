# HR & Attendance — Knowledge Base

Connexify's HR module covers three connected areas: **Attendance** (daily check-in/out and time tracking), **Leave** (applying for time off, approvals, and balances), and **HR Overview/Reports** (a dashboard and analytics summarizing both). These live under the "HR" area of the app — a manager or admin may need to turn on the **HR** section in your navigation settings if you don't see it in the sidebar yet; the features themselves are fully functional even if the menu entry is hidden.

---

## 1. Attendance

### Module Overview

The Attendance module lets every employee record when they start and stop work each day, and gives managers and admins a live, calendar-based view of who is present, late, absent, or on leave across their team. It solves a basic but critical business problem: knowing who worked, when, and for how long — without spreadsheets or manual sign-in sheets. It also automatically flags employees as "absent" overnight if they never checked in, and factors in approved leave and company holidays so the numbers stay accurate without extra manual work.

Everyone can track their own attendance. Managers and admins additionally see a "Team" view, a monthly summary table for every team member, and can export the month's attendance to CSV, edit historical records, or manually add missing entries.

### FAQ

**How do I check in and out?**
On the Attendance page (or from the check-in widget available elsewhere in the app), press **Check in**. This starts a live timer. When you're done working — or stepping away for an extended break — press **Check out**, and the app shows you a summary of how long you worked that session.

**Can I check in and out more than once in a day?**
Yes. Each check-in/check-out pair is called an **Attendance Session**. You can have multiple sessions in a single day (for example, checking out for a long break and checking back in later). The system adds up the hours from all your completed sessions for the day into your daily "Total Hours."

**What happens if I try to check in while already checked in?**
The app blocks it — you'll see "Please check out before checking in again." You must close your current session before starting a new one.

**What happens if I try to check out without checking in?**
You'll see "No active session. Please check in first." There's always exactly one open session at a time per person.

**How is "Present" vs "Late" decided?**
Your company has a configured **late threshold** (a cutoff hour and minute, e.g. 10:00 AM). If your first check-in of the day happens after that time, your status for the day is recorded as "Late." If it's before the cutoff, it's "Present." This threshold is configurable by an admin (see Leave Settings below, under "Attendance rules").

**What if I have approved leave today but still want to check in?**
Normally, checking in on a day you have approved leave is blocked with a warning ("You have an approved leave scheduled for today. Are you sure you want to check in?"). If you confirm, the system lets you check in anyway (for example, if you came in for a few hours despite being on approved leave).

**How does the nightly "mark absent" process work?**
Every night at 23:59 UTC, an automated job scans all active employees. For each person, if that day was **not** a company weekly-off day, **not** a public holiday, they **don't already have an attendance record**, and they **don't have approved leave** covering that day, the system automatically creates an "Absent" attendance log and sends them a notification. This is why you should always check in if you worked — otherwise you'll be marked absent by the following morning.

**Can I see a calendar of my attendance for the month?**
Yes — "My Attendance" shows a calendar view color-coded by day (present, late, absent, half day, on leave) plus a daily log table with your check-in/check-out times and hours worked for each day, filterable by month and year.

**How do managers view team attendance?**
Managers/admins get a "Team" tab showing a shared calendar (with daily counts of present/late/absent/on-leave across the team), a monthly summary table per employee (days present, absent, late, half-day, and total hours), and the ability to filter by department or by a specific team member. Clicking a specific day opens a detailed breakdown of every employee's status that day.

**Can attendance records be edited after the fact?**
Yes, but only by a manager or admin. They can edit an existing attendance log's status, check-in/out times, total hours, or add a note — useful for correcting mistakes (e.g., forgot to check out, biometric device failure, forgot to check in but had a valid reason). Every edit is stamped with who made it and when.

**Can a manager manually create an attendance record for someone?**
Yes — managers/admins can create a log for a date, but only for **today or earlier** (future dates are blocked), and only if no record for that person/day already exists.

**Can I export attendance data?**
Managers/admins can export a CSV of the monthly attendance report (employee name, date, check-in, check-out, hours worked, status) for the whole team or scoped group.

### Step-by-step guide: Checking in for the day

1. Open **Attendance** from the sidebar (or the check-in widget if visible in your header).
2. Press the **Check in** button.
3. If you have approved leave scheduled for today, you'll see a warning — confirm "Check in anyway" only if you intend to work despite the approved leave.
4. A live session timer starts, showing elapsed time and "In since [time]."
5. When you finish work (or want to log a break), press **Check out**. You'll see a summary of hours worked and, if you had multiple sessions that day, a breakdown of each session's duration.
6. You can check in again later the same day if needed — this starts a new session, and your daily total keeps accumulating.

### Tips & Best Practices

- Check in as soon as you start work — the late/present cutoff is based on your **first** check-in of the day.
- If you split your day (e.g., come in, leave for an appointment, come back), always check out before leaving and check back in on return — this keeps your hours accurate and avoids being seen as "still checked in" indefinitely.
- Don't forget to check out at the end of the day; an open session with no check-out won't count toward your total hours until it's closed.
- If you know you'll be out, submit a leave request in advance — it prevents the automatic "absent" mark and keeps your team's calendar accurate.

### Common Mistakes

- Forgetting to check out — hours won't be totaled for that session, and the next day you might accidentally try to check in while still "open" from the day before (if the previous day rolled over without a checkout, contact your manager to have it corrected).
- Assuming a same-day approved leave will show as "on leave" automatically if you never touch attendance — that's true, but if you do check in on a leave day, your status becomes a normal attendance record, not "on leave."
- Expecting a checked-in session late at night to still register as "Present" if it's after the late-threshold — the whole-day status is set from your *first* check-in, so a genuinely late arrival will show as "Late" regardless of how long you stay.

### Troubleshooting

**Q: Why isn't my check-in registering?**
A: The most common cause is that you already have an open session from earlier — the system won't let you check in twice without checking out in between. Also confirm you're in the correct workspace (multi-workspace users must switch to the right team context first, since attendance is tracked per workspace).

**Q: I checked in but I don't see today's status update on the dashboard.**
A: Refresh the page — some stats update on load rather than instantly. If the discrepancy persists, check whether your workspace selection at the top of the app matches the workspace you checked in under.

**Q: Why was I marked absent even though I was on leave?**
A: The nightly job only skips marking someone absent if their leave request has status **"approved"** — a still-pending request does not prevent an absent mark. Make sure your leave was approved before the day the automated check runs (just before midnight UTC). If this happens due to timing, ask a manager to edit the attendance log afterward.

**Q: Why does my check-in show as "Late" when I think I was on time?**
A: The late threshold is set company-wide (default 10:00 AM) and compares against your local check-in timestamp. Ask your HR admin what the configured cutoff is under Leave Settings → Attendance rules — it may differ from what you'd expect, or your clock/timezone may be off.

**Q: Can I fix an attendance record myself?**
A: No — only managers/admins can edit existing attendance logs or backfill missing ones. Reach out to your manager or HR admin with the correction needed.

### Permissions

- **All employees**: check in/out, view their own attendance calendar and monthly log.
- **Managers**: view team attendance (scoped to employees in their department), see the shared team calendar and per-day breakdown, edit or create attendance logs, export CSV reports — for their scoped team.
- **Company Admins**: same as managers but scoped to the entire company (all departments, all active employees).

## 2. Leave Requests (Applying for Leave)

### Module Overview

The Leave module lets employees request time off (vacation, sick leave, casual leave, unpaid leave, or any custom leave type your company has configured), see how many days they have left, and track the status of every request they've submitted. It removes the need for emailing HR or filling out paper forms, keeps a clear audit trail of who approved or rejected what and why, and automatically keeps everyone's leave balance up to date as requests are submitted, approved, rejected, or cancelled.

### FAQ

**What leave types are available?**
Leave types are configured per company (e.g., Casual Leave "CL", Sick Leave "SL", Unpaid Leave "UL"). Each type has: a name and short code, how many days per year it grants, whether it's paid, and whether unused days can carry forward into the next year (and if so, a cap on how many days can carry over).

**How do I apply for leave?**
Go to the **Leave** page, use the "Apply for leave" form: pick a leave type, choose from/to dates (or check "Half day" to request only part of a single day), add a reason, and optionally attach a supporting document (PDF/JPG/PNG, e.g., a doctor's note). The system shows you a live preview of how many actual working days that date range represents before you submit.

**Why does the "working days" preview sometimes show fewer days than my date range?**
Weekly off days (e.g., Saturday/Sunday) and company public holidays inside your selected range are automatically excluded from the day count — you're only charged leave for actual working days.

**Can I request a half day?**
Yes — check "Half day," and the request is limited to a single date, deducting 0.5 days from your balance.

**Can I apply for leave in the past?**
Generally no — from/to dates cannot be in the past. The one exception is **Sick Leave**, which can be backdated (useful when you're out sick and only submit the paperwork after returning).

**What happens if my leave dates overlap an existing request?**
The system blocks it — you can't have two pending/approved leave requests covering the same date. It'll show you which existing request(s) overlap so you can adjust your dates.

**Can I apply for leave on behalf of someone else?**
Managers and company admins can submit a leave request on behalf of one of their team members (there's an "Apply for" dropdown that defaults to "Myself" but lets a manager choose another employee).

**How do I check the status of a request I submitted?**
The **Leave Requests** page ("My leave requests") lists all your requests with filters by year and status (pending / approved / rejected / cancelled), including rejection reasons if applicable.

**Can I cancel a leave request?**
Yes, in two situations: if it's still **pending**, or if it's **approved but the start date is still in the future**. Once a leave's start date has passed, an approved request can no longer be cancelled through self-service.

**Does cancelling give my days back?**
Yes — cancelling a pending request releases the "pending" days back to your available balance; cancelling a future approved request releases the "used" days back.

**Can I see who else on my team is on leave?**
Yes, the Leave page includes a team leave calendar so you can see approved leave across your team/company alongside your own leave and public holidays — useful for planning around teammates' time off.

**What is "Insufficient balance," and can I still apply?**
If your leave type isn't Unpaid Leave and you don't have enough **available** days for the requested range, the request is blocked with an "Insufficient balance" message showing what's available vs. requested. Unpaid Leave ("UL") is exempt from this balance check — you can always apply for unpaid leave regardless of balance.

### Step-by-step guide: Applying for leave

1. Go to **Leave** in the sidebar.
2. Review your current balances at the top (available / allocated / used / pending per leave type).
3. In the "Apply for leave" form, select a **Leave type**.
4. Choose your **From** date (and **To** date, unless it's a half day).
5. Check **Half day** if you only need part of one day off.
6. Confirm the **Working days** preview matches your expectation (it automatically excludes weekends/holidays).
7. Enter a **Reason** (required).
8. Optionally attach a **Supporting document**.
9. If you see an "Overlapping leave request" warning, adjust your dates or check your existing requests first.
10. Press **Submit request**. It appears immediately as "Pending" on your Leave Requests page, and your relevant managers/admins are notified to review it.

### Glossary

- **Leave Type**: A category of leave (e.g., Casual Leave, Sick Leave, Unpaid Leave) with its own annual day allotment, paid/unpaid status, and carry-forward rules.
- **Leave Balance**: The record of how many days of a given leave type you have allocated, used, pending, and still available for a given calendar year.
- **Accrual / Carry Forward**: Some leave types allow unused days from one year to roll into the next (up to a configured maximum), rather than resetting to zero every January 1st.
- **Weekly Off**: The day(s) of the week your company treats as non-working (commonly Saturday/Sunday) — excluded automatically from leave day counts and from being marked absent.
- **Public Holiday**: A specific calendar date your company has designated as a paid day off for everyone — also excluded from leave day counts.
- **Attendance Session**: One check-in/check-out pair on a given day; a day can contain multiple sessions.

### Troubleshooting

**Q: Why is my leave balance showing a number I don't expect?**
A: Your **available** balance is always calculated as `allocated − used − pending`. If you have several requests sitting as "Pending," they already reduce your visible available balance even though they haven't been approved yet — this is intentional, to stop you from over-committing days that are awaiting approval. If a rejected/cancelled request didn't refund your balance, refresh the page; the balance updates automatically when a request's status changes.

**Q: I submitted a leave request for a date range but the app calculated fewer "days" than I expected — is that a bug?**
A: No — this is by design. Weekends (per your company's configured weekly-off days) and public holidays within your range are not counted as leave days.

**Q: Why does the app say "No working days in the selected range"?**
A: Every day in your chosen date range falls on a weekly off or public holiday — pick a range that includes at least one actual working day.

## 3. Leave Approval (For Managers & Admins)

### Module Overview

The Leave Approval module gives managers and company admins a queue of team leave requests to review, approve, or reject — with reasons captured for rejections and automatic balance updates and notifications on every decision. It ensures no leave slips through without oversight, keeps a clean audit trail, and updates employees' balances safely even if two approvers act on requests at the same time.

### FAQ

**Who sees the approval queue?**
Managers (scoped to their department) and company admins (scoped to the whole company) see the **Leave Approval** page, listing requests filtered by status, leave type, date range, or employee name search.

**How do I approve a request?**
Click **Approve** on the row. The system re-validates that there's still enough balance available (important if approvals are happening concurrently), moves the requested days from "pending" to "used," and notifies the employee (in-app notification plus an email).

**How do I reject a request?**
Click **Reject** — you must provide a rejection reason (it's required, not optional). Rejecting returns the requested days from "pending" back to "available," and the employee is notified with the reason you gave.

**Can I approve multiple requests at once?**
Yes — check the boxes next to pending requests and use **Bulk approve** to approve them all in one action; each still triggers its own individual balance update and notification.

**Is there a separate "manager approval" flow?**
Yes. In addition to the general HR-role-based approval queue, there's a direct **manager → direct report** approval path: any manager can see and approve/reject pending requests specifically from employees who report to them (based on the "Manager" field on the employee's profile), even if they aren't a formally-designated HR manager/admin role. This appears as "Pending approvals" on the HR dashboard.

**What happens if two managers try to approve the same request at once?**
The system locks the underlying balance record during approval so only one approval can succeed at a time — this prevents double-deducting or approving leave beyond what's actually available.

**Can a request be approved twice, or approved after being rejected?**
No — only requests currently in **Pending** status can be approved or rejected. Attempting to act on an already-approved/rejected/cancelled request is blocked.

**What if approving would push someone's usage past their allocated balance?**
The system re-checks available balance right before finalizing the approval and blocks it with "Insufficient leave balance" if the numbers don't add up — protecting against stale data or race conditions from simultaneous approvals.

### Step-by-step guide: Approving a team member's leave request

1. Go to **Leave Approval** (visible only to managers/admins) or check the "Pending approvals" section on the **HR Dashboard**.
2. Filter by status = "Pending" (the default) to see what needs action, optionally filtering by leave type, date range, or searching an employee's name.
3. Review the request details: employee, leave type, dates, reason, and any attached document.
4. To approve: click **Approve**. The employee's balance updates immediately and they receive a notification.
5. To reject: click **Reject**, enter a clear rejection reason (required), and confirm. The employee is notified with your reason.
6. To handle several requests quickly: select multiple pending rows with the checkboxes and click **Bulk approve**.

### Permissions

- **Employees**: cannot approve or reject any leave requests (their own included) — they can only apply and cancel their own.
- **Managers**: can approve/reject leave requests for employees scoped to their department (via the general approval queue), and additionally for their direct reports specifically (via the manager approval path), regardless of department scoping.
- **Company Admins**: can approve/reject any leave request company-wide.

### Troubleshooting

**Q: Why can't I approve a request?**
A: Either you don't have manager/admin permissions, the request is no longer "Pending" (someone already acted on it, or the employee cancelled it), or — for the manager-specific approval path — the employee isn't actually configured as your direct report (their "Manager" field doesn't point to you).

**Q: I tried to approve a request and got "Insufficient leave balance" — why, if it showed as available earlier?**
A: Balances can change between when a request was submitted and when you approve it (e.g., an admin adjusted the employee's allocation downward, or other approved leave in between consumed the balance). The system re-validates at approval time to prevent overspending an employee's balance.

**Q: I rejected a request without a reason and it failed — why?**
A: A rejection reason is mandatory — the system will not let you reject without typing one, since employees need to know why their request wasn't approved.

## 4. Leave Settings / Configuration (Admin)

### Module Overview

Leave Settings is where a company admin defines the rules the rest of the HR module runs on: what leave types exist and how many days each grants, whether individual employees' balances need manual correction, what days count as the weekly off, what dates are public holidays, and what time of day counts as "late" for attendance. Getting these settings right up front means the leave-day calculations, balances, and attendance statuses across the whole company stay accurate automatically.

### FAQ

**Who can access Leave Settings?**
Only company admins (`isCompanyAdmin` or an admin-level role) — managers do not have access to this configuration page, even though they can approve leave.

**What can I configure for a leave type?**
Name, short code (e.g., "CL", "SL", "UL"), how many days it grants per year, whether it's paid or unpaid, and whether unused days carry forward to next year (with an optional maximum number of days that can carry over).

**What's special about the "SL" (Sick Leave) code and "UL" (Unpaid Leave) code?**
The system recognizes these codes specifically: Sick Leave (code "SL") is the only type that can be applied for in the past. Unpaid Leave (code "UL") skips the available-balance check entirely, since unpaid leave isn't limited by an accrued balance.

**What happens if I delete a leave type?**
It permanently removes the leave type itself, **all** employee balances tied to it, and **all** leave requests (past and present) that used that type. This is irreversible — the confirmation dialog warns you clearly before deleting.

**How do I manually adjust someone's leave balance?**
On the "Balances" tab, select the employee, the leave type, the year, and set the new **allocated** days total (optionally with a note explaining why — e.g., "Annual policy update, correction"). This directly overwrites their allocated amount for that year; available balance recalculates automatically from allocated minus used minus pending.

**How do weekly off days work?**
Under the "Calendar" tab, toggle which days of the week (Sun–Sat) are non-working for the whole company. These days are automatically excluded from leave-day calculations and from the nightly "mark absent" job — nobody gets flagged absent or charged leave for a weekly off day.

**How do public holidays work?**
Also under "Calendar," add specific dates (with a name and optional description) as company-wide holidays. Like weekly offs, holidays are excluded from leave-day counts and from the automatic absent-marking.

**What is the "late threshold" and how do I change it?**
It's the cutoff hour/minute (default 10:00 AM) after which a first check-in of the day is marked "Late" instead of "Present." Set it under the "Calendar" tab's Attendance rules section by entering an hour (0–23) and minute (0–59), then save.

**How does the yearly balance reset work?**
On January 1st each year (00:00 UTC), an automated job resets every employee's leave balances for the new year. For leave types with carry-forward enabled, it computes the carried-over days as the smaller of (a) what was left available at year-end, or (b) the type's configured maximum carry-forward cap, and adds that to the new year's fresh allocation. Types without carry-forward simply reset to the standard days-per-year allocation, with used/pending reset to zero.

### Step-by-step guide: Configuring public holidays

1. Go to **Leave Settings** (admin only) → **Calendar** tab.
2. In the "Public holidays" panel, enter the holiday **name** (e.g., "Independence Day"), pick the **date**, and optionally add a **description**.
3. Click **Add holiday**. It appears in the list immediately and is now excluded from leave-day calculations and absence marking for that date.
4. To remove one, click **Delete** next to it and confirm.
5. Note: only one holiday can exist per date — the system blocks duplicate entries on the same day.

### Tips & Best Practices

- Configure weekly off days and public holidays for the whole year at the start of the year (or fiscal year) so leave calculations are accurate from day one.
- Set the late threshold thoughtfully — it applies company-wide and affects every employee's daily status, not per-department.
- Before deleting a leave type that's already in use, double-check — it deletes historical leave requests and balances tied to it, which may affect past reports.
- When manually adjusting a balance, always include a reason — it's stored on the record and helps explain the change later if questioned.

### Common Mistakes

- Forgetting to add a public holiday before it happens — the nightly absence job runs based on what's configured *at the time it runs*, so a late-added holiday won't retroactively fix an "Absent" mark already created; a manager will need to manually edit that attendance log afterward.
- Setting "carry forward" without a max cap — this defaults the cap to 0 if not explicitly entered, meaning effectively nothing carries forward even though the checkbox is on. Always set a max carry-forward days value when enabling carry-forward.
- Deleting a leave type mid-year that employees still have pending requests against — this removes those pending requests outright rather than resolving them first.

### Troubleshooting

**Q: Why is an employee's leave balance still wrong after I adjusted it?**
A: The "allocated" value you set is what changes; used and pending amounts are untouched by an adjustment (they reflect actual approved/pending requests). If an employee's total still looks off, check whether they have pending requests eating into the number, or confirm you adjusted the correct **year** — balances are tracked per calendar year.

**Q: I added a public holiday but an employee was still marked absent that day.**
A: The nightly job runs once, around 23:59 UTC. If the holiday was added after that day's job already ran (or after the employee had already been marked absent for a different reason, like never checking in before the holiday was configured), the mark will need to be corrected manually by editing the attendance log.

**Q: Why can't I see the Leave Settings page at all?**
A: It's restricted to company admins. Managers can approve leave and view reports, but can't change leave types, weekly offs, holidays, or the late-check-in threshold — only a full company admin account has access.

### Permissions

- **Company Admins only**: create/edit/delete leave types, adjust individual employee balances, set weekly off days, manage public holidays, and set the late-arrival threshold.
- **Managers**: cannot access any of the above configuration — their role is limited to approving/rejecting requests and viewing reports.
- **Employees**: no configuration access; they only consume the settings admins define (e.g., their leave-day counts automatically respect weekly offs and holidays).

## 5. HR Overview & Reports

### Module Overview

The HR Overview (dashboard) and HR Reports pages give a quick, visual summary of attendance and leave activity — for individuals on the dashboard, and for the whole team/company in Reports. They answer questions like "Am I checked in today?", "How much leave do I have left?", "How many approvals are waiting on me?", and, for managers, "How is my team's attendance trending this month?" and "Which leave types are being used most?"

### FAQ

**What does the HR Dashboard show me?**
A KPI strip with: today's attendance status (present/checked out/absent, with check-in/out times), your available leave balance total, either your pending-approvals count (if you're a manager/admin) or your configured leave-type count (if you're an employee), and this month's attendance summary (days present, absent, late). Below that are quick-action shortcuts to Attendance, Apply for Leave, My Leave Requests, and — for managers/admins — the Approval Queue and Leave Settings. If you're a manager/admin with pending approvals, they're also listed directly on the dashboard for one-click access.

**What does the HR Reports page show?**
For managers/admins: total leave requests, approved leave count and total working days taken, pending approvals, and average team attendance percentage for the selected month — plus charts breaking down leave requests by type and by status (approved/pending/rejected/cancelled), a detailed leave-request table, and a per-employee attendance summary table (present/absent/on-leave days and attendance percentage) for the selected month/year.

**Can employees (non-managers) see HR Reports?**
Employees see a message that "HR reports are available to managers and admins" and are directed to contact their HR admin — the detailed team analytics are manager/admin-only.

**How is "Average attendance %" on the Reports page calculated?**
It's the average number of present days across all summarized employees, divided by the number of actual working days in the selected month (excluding weekends and public holidays), expressed as a percentage.

**Can I change which month/year the Reports page shows?**
Yes — use the month/year picker at the top; all KPIs, charts, and tables update to reflect the selected period.

### Step-by-step guide: Checking your HR summary for the day

1. Open **HR Overview** (dashboard) from the sidebar.
2. Check the "Today's status" tile — it shows whether you're checked in, checked out, or still absent for the day, along with your check-in/out times if applicable.
3. Review "Available leave" for your current total balance across all leave types.
4. If you're a manager/admin, check "Pending approvals" — a non-zero count means action is needed; click through to review requests directly from the dashboard.
5. Use the "Quick actions" cards to jump straight to Attendance, Apply for leave, My leave requests, Approval queue, Leave settings, or HR Reports.

### Permissions

- **All employees**: full access to the HR Dashboard (scoped to their own attendance/leave data), plus their own pending-approvals list if they happen to be someone else's manager (direct-report approvals).
- **Managers/Admins**: additionally see team-wide pending approval counts and the full HR Reports page with company/department-scoped analytics.
- **Employees without manager/admin role**: cannot view the HR Reports charts/tables — only the message directing them to contact HR.

## Quick Glossary (All Modules)

| **Attendance Session** | One check-in-to-check-out period on a given day. A day can have multiple sessions if you check out and back in more than once. |
| **Attendance Log** | The single daily record per employee summarizing status (present/late/absent/half day), first check-in, last check-out, and total hours for that date. |
| **Leave Type** | A configured category of leave (e.g., Casual, Sick, Unpaid) with its own annual allotment, paid/unpaid flag, and carry-forward rules. |

| **Weekly Off** | The day(s) of the week (e.g., Sat/Sun) treated as non-working company-wide; excluded from leave-day counts and absence marking. |
| **Public Holiday** | A specific date designated company-wide as a paid non-working day; excluded from leave-day counts and absence marking. |
| **Late Threshold** | The configured cutoff time after which a day's first check-in marks the employee "Late" instead of "Present." |
| **Pending Approval** | A submitted leave request awaiting a manager/admin decision; its days are held in "pending" and subtracted from "available" balance until resolved. |
