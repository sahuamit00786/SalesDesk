Got it — this is the classic 131042 failure, and your diagnosis is right. Here's what's actually happening and how to fix it.

Why this happens

Billing for WhatsApp messages happens at the WABA level (190083667517741), not the app level. Your WABA was onboarded through AiSensy, so the only payment method attached to it is AiSensy's credit line — you don't own it. Error 131042 fires when:

  1. The credit line was never extended (or was revoked) for this WABA — BSPs often attach/detach credit lines as part of their managed service, and when you start sending via your own Meta app instead of theirs, some BSPs revoke the credit line since they can't bill you for that usage.
  2. The credit line has insufficient funds or AiSensy's account with Meta has a billing problem.
  3. No currency/timezone is set on the WABA's billing profile (a surprisingly common cause — billing can't initialize without it).

Note that free service conversations (replies within the 24h customer window) sometimes still go through, while template/marketing messages fail first since those always incur charges.

How to verify

Go to WhatsApp Manager → your WABA → Account tools → Payment settings (or Business Settings → WhatsApp Accounts → Payment methods). You'll see one of:

  • "Credit line: AiSensy" with a status — check if it's active
  • No payment method at all → that's your problem

Your options

Option A — Go fully independent (recommended if you're moving off AiSensy):
Add your own payment method directly to the WABA:

  1. WhatsApp Manager → Account tools → Payment settings
  2. Set your currency (INR) and timezone first
  3. Add a credit/debit card (Meta supports direct pay-as-you-go billing for Cloud API in India, with GST details)
  4. Once active, messages bill to you directly — no BSP dependency

Option B — Stay with AiSensy's credit line:
Contact AiSensy and ask them to (re)share their credit line with WABA 190083667517741. Be aware they may decline or require you to send via their platform/app, since the whole point of their credit line is billing you through their dashboard.

One caveat: a WABA can only have one payment method. If AiSensy's credit line is still attached but broken, you may need them to revoke it before you can attach your own card — or move the WABA out of any AiSensy line-of-credit sharing in Business Settings.

Given that you're already running your own app, webhook, and Cloud API integration, Option A is the clean path — otherwise you're permanently dependent on AiSensy's billing relationship for infrastructure they no longer control.
